01 · ORIGIN

Cypress, made.
— made for Cypress.

"We don't just cut hair. We cut the kind of fades that make a Tuesday feel like a fresh start, and we built a library in the lobby because reading still matters."

Rayzor Sharp started where a lot of good things start — a chair, a clipper, and a corner of Cypress that needed somewhere to get clean and feel known. Years later we're still on Cypress Creek Parkway, seven days a week, with the same idea: the cut should be sharp, the chair should be honest, and the door should be open.

We work on every grade and grain — kids in for their first sit-down, dads needing a clean line before church, women coming in for a shape-up, regulars walking through the door without saying a word because we already know.

06 · THE LIBRARY INSIDE

The One Stop
Literacy Shop.

"Sit down for a fade. Walk out with a book."

Inside Rayzor Sharp, between the second and third chair, there's a shelf — and on that shelf is a small library Rayzor Sharp built with neighbors, schools, and Cy-Fair ISD parents. Free books. Take one home, bring one back, leave one for the next kid waiting on a fade.

It started as a way to keep restless kids busy in the chair. It became something a lot bigger. We've partnered with local teachers, hosted reading days, and stocked everything from picture books to high school lit.
